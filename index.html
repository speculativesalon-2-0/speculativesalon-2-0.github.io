SPECULATIVE SALON 2.0

is a prototype of a generative installation and an online archive conceptualised and developed by Natalia Jordanova in collaboration with AI Research Scientist David Rau. This project uses machine learning to create artworks as speculative collaborations built on the artistic work of female artists who lived between the 15th and 20th centuries. As a critical tool, it deploys Image-generative AI. It learns from several Dutch digital art collections as a source to question the representation of marginalised groups within historical narratives and public archives.
Through research, development, and experimentation, the project studies the collections of Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam , Rijksmuseum,, LI-MA - Living Media Art, Nieuwe Instituut, Eye Filmmuseum, RKD Netherlands Institute for Art History
